Mentor Magic

Building a District-Wide Culture of Support

60 Minutes
5 Key Strategies
100% Actionable

About This Leadership Lab

Energize leaders to create vibrant mentoring programs

This session energizes leaders to create vibrant mentoring programs that boost member retention, enhance Pathways progress, and strengthen club communities, aligning with DCP and district goals.

Objective

Equip District 106 leaders with dynamic strategies to design, promote, and sustain mentoring programs that foster member growth and contribute to Distinguished status.

Lab Outline

Welcome & Introduction (5 minutes)

  • Greetings from Ashok Sunkavalli with an engaging prompt: Participants share one quality that makes a mentor unforgettable.
  • Highlight the PQD’s role in fostering a supportive culture to elevate member satisfaction and club success.

The Magic of Mentoring (15 minutes)

  • Explain how mentoring drives retention, Pathways completions, and DCP points, using District 106 examples (e.g., clubs with strong mentoring achieving higher member retention).
  • Share a success story of a District 106 mentor-mentee pair (real or fictional) that transformed a member’s Toastmasters journey.
  • Introduce a “Mentor Magic” framework: Connect, Guide, Inspire—to build effective mentoring programs.

Interactive Mentor Program Workshop (25 minutes)

  • Break participants into small groups to design a club or area mentoring initiative, focusing on:
    • Connect: Strategies to recruit and train mentors (e.g., mentor bootcamps or spotlighting success stories).
    • Guide: Best practices for pairing mentors with mentees based on goals and personalities.
    • Inspire: Ways to recognize mentoring achievements and create a culture of appreciation.
  • Groups pitch their initiatives in a “Magic Pitch” format, with Ashok offering feedback tied to district-wide member growth and Pathways completion goals.
  • Share a “Mentor Magic Toolkit” with templates for mentor applications and training materials.

Spreading the Magic in District 106 (10 minutes)

  • Discuss how Area and Division Directors can encourage and support mentoring across their areas, fostering a district-wide culture of collaboration and support.
  • Provide a takeaway: a “District-Wide Mentoring Guide” with tips for scaling programs and celebrating mentor success.

Q&A and Call to Action (5 minutes)

  • Address questions on mentoring challenges or strategies.
  • Challenge participants to launch one new mentoring initiative in their club or area within 30 days and share their progress with the PQD.

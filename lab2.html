Club Quality Quest

Crafting Epic Meetings That Win Members & Awards

60 Minutes
5 Key Strategies
100% Actionable

About This Leadership Lab

Empower leaders to elevate club meeting quality through innovative, engaging formats

This session empowers leaders to elevate club meeting quality through innovative, engaging formats that enhance member satisfaction, attract new members, and maximize DCP points.

Objective

Provide District 106 leaders with bold, practical tools to create standout club meetings, fostering retention and growth to support the district’s Distinguished goals.

Lab Outline

A comprehensive 60-minute session packed with actionable strategies

Welcome & Introduction

5 minutes

Greetings from Ashok Sunkavalli with a lively prompt: Participants share one adjective for the “ultimate” Toastmasters meeting. Emphasize the Program Quality Director’s role in elevating club experiences to drive member retention and district success.

The Club Quality Connection

15 minutes

Explain how exceptional meetings drive member satisfaction, retention, and DCP points (e.g., membership growth, Pathways completions). Share a success story of a District 106 club that transformed attendance with creative meetings (real or fictional). Introduce the “Club Quality Quest” framework: three pillars—Innovate, Include, Inspire—for epic meetings.

Interactive Meeting Design Lab

25 minutes

Break participants into small groups to design a “next-level” club meeting based on the framework: Innovate: Try new formats like themed meetings. Include: Ensure diverse roles, voices, and participation. Inspire: Add motivational elements like guest speaker showcases or Pathways milestone celebrations. Groups pitch their meeting designs in a fast-paced “Quest Pitch” format, with Ashok offering feedback tied to DCP impact. Share a “Club Quality Checklist” to help leaders assess and improve their meetings.

Spreading the Quest Across District 106

10 minutes

Brainstorm ways to promote high-quality meetings district-wide, such as a “Club Quality Challenge” where clubs share their best meeting ideas at area or division events. Discuss how Area and Division Directors can coach clubs to align meeting innovations with DCP goals. Provide a takeaway: a “Club Quality Quest Guide” with meeting templates and DCP tracking tips.

Q&A and Call to Action

5 minutes

Address questions on implementing or scaling these ideas. Challenge participants to pilot one new meeting format in their club and share results with the PQD to build momentum for Distinguished District status.

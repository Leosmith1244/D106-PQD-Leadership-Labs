Evaluation Elevation

Transforming Member Growth with Exceptional Feedback Skills

60 Minutes
5 Key Strategies
100% Actionable

About This Leadership Lab

Empower leaders to deliver evaluations that inspire and guide members

This session empowers leaders to deliver evaluations that go beyond a simple summary, providing constructive feedback that inspires and guides members to new heights in their public speaking and leadership journey.

Objective

Provide District 106 leaders with the skills and confidence to deliver exceptional evaluations that drive member engagement, educational awards, and overall club success.

Lab Outline

Welcome & Introduction (5 minutes)

  • Greetings from Ashok Sunkavalli with a focused prompt: Participants share one word describing the impact of a great evaluation.
  • Highlight the PQD’s role in promoting a culture of high-quality evaluations to drive member retention and overall club success.

The Power of Great Feedback (15 minutes)

  • Explain how high-quality evaluations directly impact member growth and retention (Pathways completions, retention).
  • Share a story of a District 106 member who grew through impactful feedback (real or fictional).
  • Introduce an “Evaluation Elevation” framework: Specific, Actionable, Motivational—to deliver transformative feedback.

Interactive Evaluation Workshop (25 minutes)

  • Break participants into small groups to practice delivering evaluations using a sample speech video or live volunteer speech:
    • Specific: Focus on clear, targeted feedback tied to speech objectives.
    • Actionable: Provide practical suggestions for improvement.
    • Motivational: Inspire confidence with positive reinforcement.
  • Groups share their evaluation techniques in a dynamic “Feedback Face-Off,” with Ashok offering insights tied to Pathways and DCP goals.
  • Share an “Evaluation Playbook” with tips and sample feedback structures.

Spreading Evaluation Excellence in District 106 (10 minutes)

  • Brainstorm ways to promote evaluation skills district-wide, such as hosting an “Evaluation Extravaganza” contest for clubs.
  • Discuss how Area and Division Directors can coach clubs to prioritize quality evaluations to boost member engagement.
  • Provide a takeaway: an “Evaluation Elevation Guide” with templates and training ideas.

Q&A and Call to Action (5 minutes)

  • Address questions on evaluation challenges or techniques.
  • Challenge participants to conduct an evaluation training session in their club within 60 days and share results with the PQD.
